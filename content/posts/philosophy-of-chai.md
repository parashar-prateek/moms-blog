There's a reason chai is not just a drink in our culture — it's an emotion, a ritual, a conversation starter.

Every morning, as I stand in the kitchen watching the milk rise and the tea leaves release their colour, I think about patience. Chai teaches you that. You can't rush it. Too much heat and it boils over. Too little and the flavours never bloom.

My mother used to say, *"Zindagi chai jaisi hai — thoda sabr chahiye, thoda meetha, aur bahut saara pyaar."* (Life is like chai — it needs a little patience, a little sweetness, and a whole lot of love.)

Here's my recipe for the perfect cup:

- One cup water, one cup milk
- Two spoons loose leaf Assam tea
- One crushed elaichi
- A tiny piece of adrak
- Sugar to taste

Bring the water to a boil with the spices. Add tea leaves. Let it simmer until the colour deepens. Add milk. Watch it rise — and just before it spills over, take it off the heat.

That moment of almost-spilling? That's where the magic lives. In life too.
