The British East India Company entered India via Calcutta with the intention to secure exclusive trading rights in the Indian Ocean, breaking the Portuguese and Dutch monopolies! This trading company later ruled a large part of India from 1757 to 1857, transforming itself from a trading body into a colonial administration after the Battle of Plassey in 1757 — fought between the East India Company led by Lord Clive and the last independent Nawab of Bengal, Siraj-ud-Daulah!

The East India Company won this battle due to the betrayal of Mir Jafar, who was the commander of the Nawab's army! Once the Nawab lost the Battle of Plassey, the rise of the East India Company began. The Company took over full political and military control in Bengal, and the doors were opened for colonial rule in India.

The East India Company ruled India from 1757 to 1857, and after this the British Crown rule began — they ruled India from 1858 to 15th August 1947. The total duration of British rule was nearly 200 years (1757 to 1947).

During these two hundred years of British rule, lakhs of Indians were transported as labourers to other British colonies across the globe against their wishes. This was FORCED transportation of labourers, resulting in lakhs of families being separated from their loved ones. Innocent people of India faced profound hardship and suffering. The British exploited India economically! The Indian subcontinent, which was a global economic hub, collapsed and turned into one of the world's poorest nations! Indian industries were downgraded as machine-made goods were promoted! The British literally looted India and left behind scars of their atrocities and destruction in each and every field!

We have not yet fully recovered from their shadow — India's judiciary and education system are still largely influenced by British colonial policies! The government is trying to decolonize these systems by introducing certain reforms in criminal laws and in the National Education Policy (NEP). Let's see how impactful these reforms will be.

**No Indian had ever opted for these 200 years of British rule!** The British imposed a system of forced labour on Indians. Slavery in British India was abolished by the Indian Slavery Act of 1843, but even after its abolition, there was hardly any improvement in the condition of Indians! After decades of suffering and hardship, India finally gained independence from British rule on 15th August 1947!!

Please note that use of technology under checks and balances can be useful for humanity, but blind dependence on technology can be harmful!
